# Problem Statement — Deep-Tech Pulse

## The Problem
The agentic coding landscape moves fast — new models, tools, techniques, and breakthroughs emerge daily. A small team trying to stay current faces information overload across dozens of sources (Twitter/X, YouTube, tech blogs, tool changelogs, research papers). Knowledge sharing happens through conversation, but conversations are ephemeral and don't scale — when someone is busy, they miss updates. There's no persistent, curated view of "what's happening right now in agentic coding."

## Evidence
- The team currently relies on talking to each other to stay up to speed — this is the primary knowledge sharing mechanism (confirmed by human)
- No dashboard or aggregation tool exists for the team today (confirmed by human)
- The team wants a "single place to see what is going on, what to look out for, and the new best way to work" (direct quote from human input)
- When anyone is free, they should be able to "just look at it" — implying the current approach requires active effort to gather information

## Opportunity
When this is solved, the team gains:
1. **Time savings** — no more manually scanning multiple sources, RSS feeds, and YouTube channels
2. **Equal access** — every team member has the same visibility into AI developments, regardless of who they talked to today
3. **Actionable intelligence** — urgency labels ("Use Now") immediately surface things the team should adopt or test
4. **Tool awareness** — Tool Spotlight tracks what's changing in Cursor, Claude, Devin, etc. — the actual tools the team uses daily
5. **Historical context** — content persists in the database, building a searchable archive of agentic coding developments (v2: full-text search)

## Root Causes
1. **Source fragmentation** — AI news is scattered across 20+ sources with no single aggregator focused on agentic coding
2. **Signal-to-noise ratio** — general AI news (chatbots, image generation, policy) drowns out agentic coding-specific content
3. **Ephemeral knowledge sharing** — conversations don't persist; someone who was in a meeting misses the update
4. **No curation** — nobody has time to curate and post links consistently (confirmed: human wants "fully automated")
5. **Generic tools don't filter correctly** — RSS readers and news aggregators don't understand the difference between "generic AI" and "agentic coding"
